A 3-star AC is one that has an Energy Efficiency Ratio (EER) of 2.9 to 3.09, while a 5-star AC has an EER of 3.3 and above. This difference means that a 3-star AC would cost you approximately INR 580 per month in electricity charges, while a 5-star AC of equivalent capacity would cost you around INR 500 for the same period. This means that the 5-star AC is actually cheaper in the long run.

Energy Production Levels

Here is a huge discrepancy between the countries that use the most energy and the ones that use the least. Plus, the countries that produce the most energy aren’t necessarily the ones that use the most. Here is a list of the five countries that use and produce that most energy in the world..

India

With over 1.2 billion people in the country, India is another of the most populous nations in the world. While it’s the fourth largest energy consumer in the world, it is much lower down on the list in energy use per capita. Its energy use comes mostly from the sheer number of people in India. India has been rapidly developing in recent years, with more urbanization and more people gaining access to electricity.

USA

The U.S. uses the most energy per capita in the world. America’s oil, coal and natural gas consumption is the most per person of any country in the world, using 21 percent of the world’s energy. While China actually uses more, it has a lot more people to provide power for. The U.S. is second in the world in energy production. President Obama signed the Paris Agreement last year in an effort to leave office with a plan to address climate change, but Donald Trump’s policies and statements have led many to believe he will only increase the U.S.’s production and consumption.

China

China is the most populous country in the world, with over 1.3 billion people. It’s a lot of people to provide power for, making China the top energy consumer in the world. Still, while the country as a whole uses the most energy, it’s not the most per capita, meaning it uses less energy per person that the United States. China’s large population plays a part in its energy usage. Its heavy reliance on manufacturing as a part of the national economy also uses a large portion of energy. China was also the top energy producer in the world in 2015.

Japan

This island nation also uses a lot of energy. It has been neck and neck with India for the fourth spot for years. Japan’s overall usage is higher than that of both France and Germany, although the latter two each use more per capita than Japan does. Japan is also one of the world’s top energy producers.

Russia

Russia was third in the world in energy consumption in 2015, using 6 percent of the world’s energy. It is also the third largest energy producer. Russia’s cold climate and manufacturing industries account for its energy use. Although Russia is the world’s third largest consumer, it doesn’t consume nearly as much energy as the United States or China.

ENERGY STAR certification

Earning the ENERGY STAR means products meet strict energy efficiency guidelines set by the US Environmental Protection Agency. By choosing ENERGY STAR certified heating and cooling equipment and taking steps to optimize its performance, you can enhance the comfort of your home while saving energy. Saving energy helps you save money on utility bills and protect our climate by helping prevent harmful carbon pollution and reducing other greenhouse gases.

About Standards & Labeling Program

The Objectives of Standards & Labeling Program is to provide the consumer an informed choice about the energy saving and thereby the cost saving potential of the marketed household and other equipment.
